Our Story

After 30 years of above average health and thinking I'd live forever, an emergency biopsy confirmed I had cancerous brain tumors.

From the biopsy to seven months of chemo and a stem cell transplant (all before and during COVID-19), my biggest concern was not death, but rather, no longer being a positive presence on the lives of the ones I love. A positive mindset is key to many things in life because as they say, "worry only prepares you for failure". So, my message for bad situations despite the emotional toll it takes to have hope and "fake it until you do – they make it" or at least try).

My cancer experience caused mild epilepsy and after experiencing a few seizures, I know life can "turn off" without even knowing it. The unexpected losses of family and friends also inspired me to create something simple, unique and allow for moving to transend time without complex legal requirements. I want to change the way we think about the inevitable end and grieving for our self and others. I hope Legacy Cloud helps families stress less by knowing where to start when it comes to handling after necessities. I hope too that people leave messages to be shared and inspire those who are left behind.

In the spirit of helping and inspiring hope, YourEkko has teamed up with Ohio State's Comprehensive Cancer Center – James Cancer Hospital and Solove Research Institute to support the vision of a cancer-free world! I was treated at the OSUCCC – James, so it feels good to give back and help others who will be in my position or avoid the position entirely! YourEkko is donating $25 per month and $1 per Ekkoes package indefinitely, as well as any unclaimed monetary gifts. If you too would like to contribute directly to the OSUCCC – James, you can do so by clicking the link below.

- Zach Spring
